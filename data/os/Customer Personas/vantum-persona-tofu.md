# Persona — The Adjacent Fractional

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU · Awareness Audience
**Buying Window:** Not buying — wrong stage or no driver
**Highest Value:** Amplification, not revenue

---

## Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Fractional CMO, CFO, COO, CRO, or aspiring |
| **Tenure** | 0 yrs (aspiring) to 20+ yrs (winding down) |
| **Client Load** | 0 to 3 — often inconsistent |
| **Weekly Hours** | 15–35 by choice or by lack of demand |
| **Team** | 0 |
| **Tool Spend** | $50–$300 / month (deliberately lean) |
| **Decision Power** | Sole — but rarely buying anything new |
| **Annual Revenue** | $0 to $200K |

## Background

They are in the fractional ecosystem but not in the moment when the ecosystem hurts. Some are between engagements — they had clients, they don't right now, they're rebuilding the pipeline. Some are deliberately designed for low capacity — they decided years ago that 25 hours a week and three retainers was the right shape of life. Some are calm veterans who've seen worse and rolled with it. Some are advisory-only, handing off the work after strategy. Some are aspiring — they haven't taken on a client yet. They're learning a coaching program first.

What they share is reach. They post on LinkedIn. They live inside the communities the BOFU operators live in. They share content they recognize as honest. They sit on panels and podcasts. They sponsor the conversation about fractional work. They know the language fluently because they speak it daily — even if they're not currently experiencing the acute version of the pain.

### The Core Insight

**They are not the buyer. They are the amplifier. Their highest value to you is the BOFU customer who follows them on LinkedIn.**

If you target this persona expecting conversion, you'll be disappointed. If you target them expecting amplification — shares, comments, "tag a fractional friend" — you'll find them remarkably generous and reliably loud.

---

## The five sub-patterns

Unlike BOFU and MOFU, this is not a single character. It's a coalition. Five distinct sub-patterns coexist here, bound by one shared trait: not currently in the moment of acute capacity pain.

1. **Between engagements.** Had clients, don't right now. Pipeline anxious. The fractional version of unemployment. Their top-of-mind pain is finding clients, not managing them.
2. **The lifestyle operator.** Deliberately runs 20–35 hours a week. Three retainers max. Designed the business backward from the life. Genuinely happy. Will not buy anything that smells like "more."
3. **The calm veteran.** Has clients, has tenure, has seen worse. Denies all the strain language when shown the pitch deck. They're not lying. They just genuinely don't feel it.
4. **The strategic advisor.** Sells strategy, hands off execution. Doesn't generate the same delivery pressure as the BOFU operator. Sometimes winding down their practice.
5. **The aspiring fractional.** Hasn't taken on a paid client yet. Learning a coaching program, building a website, planning. Their pain is starting, not scaling.

**Disqualifier:** Full-time employee with a CFO-adjacent title at an accounting firm. Not an independent fractional. Disqualified at the role gate — do not nurture.

## A Tuesday across two sub-patterns

### Sub-pattern 2 · The lifestyle operator

- **9:30 AM** — They open LinkedIn first. Coffee. They scroll. They post a thoughtful comment on a fractional operator's piece about pricing. They check email — three inquiries from the last two weeks, none urgent.
- **11:00 AM** — One client call. Goes well. They've been at this for fifteen years; the call is easy.
- **12:30 PM** — They consider whether to redo their pricing page. They open the website builder, make a small change, close it. They take the afternoon off. They tell themselves they're being intentional. They are.

### Sub-pattern 1 · The between-engagements operator

- **9:00 AM** — They open the laptop and refresh the inbox five times in twenty minutes. They post on LinkedIn — a piece about feast-or-famine pipeline. They mean it.
- **11:30 AM** — They send three cold messages. They review their pipeline spreadsheet. Nothing's moved in a week.
- **2:00 PM** — They wonder if their positioning is wrong. They consider taking a course. They scroll LinkedIn some more. They like and share a post about the 4th client problem. They have one client.

Both Tuesdays end without anyone buying anything. Both Tuesdays involved sharing fractional-pain content with thousands of LinkedIn followers.

## Goals (theirs, mostly not Vantum's)

This is the single most important thing to internalize about this persona: **their top-of-mind problem is pipeline, not capacity.** They want clients. They want a steady stream. They want to escape the bumpy feast-famine cycle. If you target them with "tame your week" messaging, they'll ignore it. Their week is fine.

- Get more clients (for those between engagements or pipeline-anxious)
- Protect the lifestyle they designed (for those running deliberately lean)
- Position themselves distinctively in a crowded fractional market
- Stop sounding like every other AI-generated LinkedIn post
- Wind down gracefully (for those exiting)
- Land their first client (for the aspiring)

## Frustrations

- Pipeline goes bumpy — long quiet stretches punctuated by clusters of inquiries
- Marketing complexity has exploded — "SEO is its own monster, digital marketing its own beast"
- They keep getting confused with junior consultants on LinkedIn
- Strategy-only work loses to operators who execute
- Self-promotion is the work they keep dropping
- They know they should be doing outbound. They aren't.

---

## In their words

> "It's pretty bumpy. I can go long periods without any new client inquiries, and then I'll get a couple at a time. I'd like a more steady state of new client opportunities."

> "Feast or famine — you get focused doing the work, you drop the self-promotion, and then you run out of work and you're starving. By the time you pick up your head, you're back at zero again."

> "There was a date like 10 years ago I could have been a one-man marketing show. Now everything is a specialty."

> "A lot of folks go business goals first. I did it backwards — I looked at life goals first, figured out what I'm okay with and what I'm not, and then made the business fit within that."

> "I'm happy working 35, no more than 40 hours a week. I'm not looking to burn the midnight oil."

> "The biggest challenge in this space is to actually get clients. When you get clients, you don't say no. You figure it out to make it happen."

Notice the register: **calm, articulate, often abstract.** They talk *about* the fractional condition rather than from inside it. This is the audible signature of TOFU — the BOFU persona uses the same words with different urgency.

## Their stack today (deliberately simple)

They have not built a sophisticated system because they don't need one. Three clients fit in a Google Doc. Marketing fits on LinkedIn. Invoicing fits in a spreadsheet. The lean stack is a feature, not a gap.

- LinkedIn (primary everything)
- Notion or Google Doc
- Basic calendar (Google or Cal.com)
- ChatGPT (casual use)
- Email back-and-forth (no scheduler)
- No CRM
- No custom agents
- Maybe a Substack

Selling them tools is like selling a treadmill to someone who walks five miles a day by choice. The problem they have isn't the absence of the tool.

## What could move them (promote out of TOFU)

Most TOFU operators will not cross any of these thresholds in the next 12 months. They are not nurture targets in the traditional sense. But a quarterly or annual revisit should test for these:

- **Landing 3+ retainers simultaneously** after a dry spell — promotes to MOFU
- **A career inflection** — going from advisor to executor
- **Hiring a contractor or VA** — adds coordination complexity that breaks lean setups
- **Saying yes to a 4th client** because the money was too good — and immediately regretting it
- **A "fractional credibility gap" moment** forcing them to productize the offer
- **A new client load that exceeds what their Google Doc can hold**

**Critically:** engagement with TOFU content does NOT predict promotion. They will engage, share, comment — and still not buy. The only signal that matters is **a new active client load**. Track that, not email opens.

## How to talk to them — content for amplification, not conversion

The goal of TOFU content is not to sell them anything. The goal is to give them something to share. Their BOFU peers — the buyers — see it because they saw it in their feed.

### Headlines and hooks

- **Universal high-share:** "You know exactly what you should be working on this week. You also know you won't get to it."
- **Pattern naming:** "Feast or famine isn't a stage. It's a system problem."
- **Anti-generic hook:** "The 4 words every fractional has said: I'll get to it."
- **Pain-aware (verbatim):** "I haven't lost a client yet, but I know it's going to happen."
- **Carousel format:** "30 fractional operators. One pattern."

### What NOT to do

- **Don't address them as the buyer.** They aren't. Address the BOFU buyer; let TOFU amplify.
- **Don't lecture** about "what fractionals need" — they'll close the tab.
- **Don't use generic startup language** ("save time," "be productive"). Use specific, evidence-based pain.
- **Don't confuse pipeline content (their pain) with capacity content (BOFU pain)** on the same channel — pick one per post.
- **Don't pitch.** Pitching loses their amplification. It also kills their trust permanently.
- **Don't pay to reach them.** Paid spend on TOFU does not convert. Save the budget for BOFU and MOFU.

## Where to find them

Organic only.

**Reach them on:**
- LinkedIn organic (primary)
- Fractionals United, Savvy, Chief
- Substack on fractional life
- Podcast guesting
- Carousels on LinkedIn
- Short video (Reels / LinkedIn video)
- Industry-specific Slack groups
- Community panels

**Never:** Paid acquisition, cold outreach, mass email, retargeting. Each of these converts TOFU at near-zero and burns trust.

---

## Who is not this persona

- **The exhausted 4-client operator** with acute strain — that's BOFU, sell now, don't waste them on awareness content
- **The veteran with a refined system** who insists they've solved capacity — that's MOFU, nurture them
- **The full-time CFO** at an accounting firm using "fractional" as a job title — disqualified entirely
- **The strategy-only advisor** permanently winding down — disqualified
- **Someone who has never done any fractional work** and never will — they're a follower, not a persona

The risk with TOFU is treating engagement as buying intent. It isn't. The only meaningful signal that a TOFU has moved up the funnel is a change in their active client load — not how many of your posts they liked.
