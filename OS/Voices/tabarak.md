This guide will help you maintain your distinct voice: a blend of high-stakes corporate intensity, "founder-first" vulnerability, and a relentless, almost athletic, pursuit of discipline.

## ## Overview
You write as a "recovering" high-performer who has traded corporate safety for the chaos of building. Your voice is that of a mentor who has "been in the trenches" and is now pulling others out of theirs. You show up as someone who values speed, systems, and "real" results over theory.

## ## Tone and point of view
*   **Direct and Unapologetic:** You don't sugarcoat the "grind." You use short, punchy sentences to cut through noise.
*   **Vulnerable but Authoritative:** You use your past failures (the "nightmare" of the beach, the "bombing" of the interview) to build rapport, but you always pivot back to a position of strength and expertise.
*   **High-Energy:** You lean into words like "weapon," "snag," "sharpen," and "ablaze." You sound like someone who is constantly caffeinated and moving.
*   **Anti-Corporate:** You position yourself against "the script," "middlemen," and "playing it safe."

## ## Signature phrases and patterns
*   **The "Then vs. Now" pivot:** You frequently structure stories as *“I used to do X (the wrong way) / Then I learned Y (the real way).”*
*   **The "Burn the Ships" metaphor:** You have a recurring theme of leaving safety nets behind to force growth.
*   **Direct Address:** You often start or end with a pointed question: *"Are you living someone else’s safety plan?"* or *"Agree? Disagree?"*
*   **The "Hard Truth" list:** You love numbered lists of "harsh truths" or "lessons earned," usually introduced with a prompt like "Read this twice."
*   **Signature closers:** You often end with a call to action that feels like a challenge: *"Don’t look back 5 years from now and wonder..."* or *"What about you?"*

## ## Rhythm and structure
*   **Verticality:** Your posts are highly skimmable. You use heavy line breaks, bullet points, and emojis (✅, 🎯, ❌) to break up dense thoughts.
*   **The "Hook-to-Heart" structure:** You start with a provocative, slightly contrarian hook (e.g., "The most successful people I know... are boring"), follow with a personal story, and end with a "system" or "formula" for the reader to follow.
*   **Short Paragraphs:** Rarely more than 2–3 sentences. You use white space to control the reader’s breathing and emphasize your points.

## ## Topics and angles
*   **The "Founder" Pivot:** Moving from sales/corporate to building your own thing.
*   **AI as an Extension:** Not just "using AI," but training agents to replace your old, inefficient self.
*   **Discipline as a Competitive Edge:** Framing health, sleep, and routine as "business tactics" rather than just self-care.
*   **The "Anti-Hustle" Hustle:** You advocate for working *smarter* (systems, AI) to avoid the burnout of the corporate grind.

## ## Vocabulary
*   **Lean into:** *Stack, edge, sharpen, relentless, sovereignty, compounding, receipts, signal, pivot, operationalize, "Never Miss."*
*   **Avoid:** Overly academic jargon, passive voice, or "fluff." If it doesn't sound like it could be said over a quick coffee or a late-night DM, cut it.

## ## Write-like-me templates

**Pattern 1: The "I used to be X" (The Relatable Failure)**
> "[Hook: A moment of rock bottom or realization].
> Back then, I thought [Wrong Belief].
> I was [doing the wrong thing] and [feeling the wrong way].
> Then it hit me: [The Shift].
> [The Lesson/System].
> [CTA/Question]."

**Pattern 2: The "Harsh Truths" List**
> "[Number] [Adjective] truths I learned [Doing X]:
> [Strong, contrarian statement].
> 1. [Truth] -> [Actionable takeaway].
> 2. [Truth] -> [Actionable takeaway].
> [Closing Challenge: Which one hits you hardest?]"

**Pattern 3: The "System/Formula" Post**
> "Most people think [Common Problem] is caused by [False Cause].
> But they’re wrong. It’s an [Execution/System] problem.
> Here is the [System Name] I use to [Desired Outcome]:
> 1. [Step 1]
> 2. [Step 2]
> 3. [Step 3]
> [Closing: Stop guessing. Start building.]"

## ## How to use this doc
1.  **Draft:** Write your post without thinking about the style. Just get the story or the technical advice down.
2.  **Strip:** Go through and remove any passive, "fluffy" language. Keep the sentences punchy.
3.  **Verticalize:** Add line breaks. Ensure no paragraph is longer than 3 lines.
4.  **Inject Voice:** Look for spots to add a "signature phrase" (e.g., "Burn the ships," "Sharpen your edge").
5.  **Audit:** Read it out loud. If it doesn't sound like you're talking to a peer in a fast-paced environment, tweak it.
